Paris

Paris is the capital and most populous city of France. Situated on the Seine River, in the north of the country. Since the 17th century, Paris has been one of the world's major centres of finance, diplomacy, commerce, culture, fashion, and gastronomy. For its leading role in the arts and sciences, as well as its early and extensive system of street lighting, in the 19th century, it became known as the City of Light.

Lyon

Lyon is spread over a narrow peninsula between the Rhône and Saône rivers and on their opposite banks. A zone of factory and residential suburbs encircles the city. On the right bank of the Saône, Vieux Lyon (Old Lyon) remains as one of the finest surviving architectural complexes of the Renaissance era.

Bordeaux

Bordeaux, located in southwestern France, is famed as the "Wine Capital of the World," celebrated for its vineyards producing renowned wines. The city's UNESCO-listed historic district, the "Port of the Moon," showcases elegant 18th-century architecture along the Garonne River. Bordeaux boasts a vibrant cultural scene, with museums like the CAPC Museum of Contemporary Art, and offers culinary delights featuring local specialties. The picturesque riverfront, including landmarks like the Place de la Bourse, adds to Bordeaux's unique charm.
